Project Overview

Brief Description

Blacksite Theta is a University of Wisconsin-Whitewater capstone project that I was lucky enough to be able to work on. The game used an alien creature imbedded into your characters arms to give you control over unique abilities based around growth and retraction of your arms, super strength, and electricity. You had to navigate and survive through a series of puzzles to reach the ending of the game.

Contributions and Responsibilities

I was working primarily in the tech team of the Blacksite Theta, though I also did work in the design of the project. A majority of my time during development was spent on the character controller, as it needed to work well alone while also being compatible with the movement abilities that player had. I was also contributing to the abilities themselves, as I had fully developed the growth and retraction abilities, the strength rebound and the shattering of the smashed crates, and heavily refactored the electric abilities to work with larger systems.

Challenges Faced

  1. There were many challenges during development, some of which were quick to solve but the ones that stand out were far larger. We needed to allow for abilities to cancel the character movement when they controllled the movement with the Retraction, Rebound, and Dash abilities.
  2. Another large problem was the animations of the player, as we wanted the arms to move specifically to the place where they were being used, such as actually hitting the crates that are being smashed. Lastly, grounding animations and airborne animations were tricky, as my controller wasn't created with ramped surfaces being considered as far as animations were considered. This led to the airborne animation triggerring falsely when walking up or down a ramped surface. Unfortunately, I was unable to perfect the ramp issue when development finished.

Lessons Learned

  1. Luckily, I was learning the SOLID design principles in another class during this project, which made it possible for me to use those lessons throughout development. I learned how important it is to decouple code to keep systems separate and allow for expansion. Originally, we only had two player powersets in mind, the growth and strength powers, and later added the electric abilities. I learned how to develop my character controller to allow for more abilities to be added without needing to modify the code of it for each movement ability using events.
  2. I also had been learning the basics of inverse kinematics for another class working on a virtual reality full body avatar. That knowledge made it easy for me to implement the arm ability animations in such a way to where they would be aimed correctly at the point of interaction.
