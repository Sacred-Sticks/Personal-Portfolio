Project Overview

Brief Description

A Statue of Time was the first project that I had programmed entirely on my own. It was a class project that lasted about half the semester. You control a character that was cursed to turn to stone and view his statues but he uses this curse to escape the tower he is trapped in. By turning to stone, your statues can press buttons, weigh down pulleys, and give access to difficult to reach locations.

Contributions and Responsibilities

I provided all of the programming for A Statue of Time using the Unity Engine and the pre-existing Unity components. This project was created with a team of 2, where I initially served as the programmer and my partner would develop the art, but we realized we needed someone to make levels. I took a major role in level development and developed thirteen of the total levels.

Challenges Faced

  1. One of the biggest problems was level development. I had never been able to creatively design levels that could make a player reconsider their abilities and do something new. I needed to find a way to make players think about their ability options in new ways that they perhaps hadn't considered prior.
  2. On the tech side, I had a major problem that was unable to be solved during development time, but if I were to rework it today I would have the solution. If you were to walk on a pulley and walk over the edge of it while barely staying on it, the pulley would start pulling down for no reason. I was able to slow it down and make it less noticable, but this project was before I learned any of the Unity Physics Engine so I wasn't using any joints which is what caused the bug.

Lessons Learned

  1. When working on the level design, I thought about what I could do to create unique use cases for the abilitites. One of the levels I'm most proud of involves a button that opens the exit but you aren't able to reach the button without holding another button down. However, you only have one statue to use in this level, which makes the solution quite a sparking moment for the player when they discover it. To come up with this, I thought about the mechanics and considered how they might be able to interact with each other, which lead to the piston interacting with a statue which lead to the solution for this level.
  2. Were I to create this game now, I would use a configurable joint for the pulleys isntead of manually controlling the position through a direct modification of the transform values. Using the joint would solve this because I would lock all the rotations and positions except for the y position. Then, I would simply modify the maxDistance value based on the mass of the rigidbodies on top of it, and I would configure the settings on the joint to prevent rapid movement, making all movement be at the same speed.
