Project Overview

Brief Description

The Ecology Simulation is a project that I made as a part of my software engineering course during university. This is less of a game and more of a simulation meant to be used for educational purposes to better understand how small changes in populations could modify the outcome of an ecosystem.

Contributions and Responsibilities

I was the primary developer in this project. I had six other teammates but I was the only person with prior experience using the Unity Engine, so I did end up with a majority of the work. I created the AI for the foxes as well as the carrots and walked my teammate through development of the rabbit. I also developed the interactions between the different species and the user interface.

Challenges Faced

  1. While working on the fox AI, I needed a way for foxes to see the rabbits in front of them rather than simply targeting the closest rabbit, as that rabbit could be behind it.
  2. Throughout development, I had to ensure that the systems developed by everyone on the team would work cleanly together. This ultimately cost a lot of time, but was worth it in the end.

Lessons Learned

  1. Ultimately, the system would collect all the potential prey within a circle around it, then filter that list down according to the angle made by the vector connecting the two mobs and the forward of the hunter. This way, I could ensure that animals could only make decisions based on what would be realistically seen by them while also allowing different species to see different things. We used that specifically to make the rabbits have a much wider angle of vision than the foxes because they have eyes on the sides of their heads as a prey, whereas the foxes had eyes in the front as predators.
  2. This was my first project where I served as a project lead. I learned to take into consideration the experience of everyone in the team rather than basing project specifications on my own skills and abilities because ultimately I cannot expect them to match up. My most valuable lesson was to be patient with some team members as they may still need to pick up the software and understand it.
